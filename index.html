ABOUT ME

Umairah Radzi is a 26 year old self taught traditional fine art artist born and raised in Perak with an interest in illuminated manuscript, calligraphy and Islamic Illumination art called Tezhip. Her passion lies in intricate geometrical and traditional motifs from SEA and the Middle East. Umairahâ€™s work references old Illuminated Manuscripts, an art form that in modern time is taken for granted. She strives to balance between tradition and modernity to tell stories, histories and philosophies through her arts. Starting out in 2018 as a traditional artist, she slowly explores her art journey and decided to start being a full time artist and dedicated herself to her crafts. When she started to receive clients and collectors from overseas she realised that there are actually many people out there who are interested and will appreciate the local arts and crafts.

These hand-painted pieces were inspired by the old Turkish, Persian and Malay Quran Manuscripts. They are adorned with traditional Songket and Malay motifs from Perak specifically Tekat Benang Emas. The huge paintings were done on 4ft x 3ft canvases.
